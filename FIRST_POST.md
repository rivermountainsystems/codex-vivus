# I Talked to AI for Three Years. Then I Made It Read All of It Back to Me.

4,947 conversations. 37.7 million words. Three years.

That's how much I've talked to ChatGPT and Claude since April 2023. More than most people write in a lifetime. More than the complete works of Shakespeare, Tolkien, and the King James Bible — combined, times four.

I didn't plan to do this. Nobody does. You start with one question. Then another. Then it's 3am and you're 40 messages deep into how the Federal Reserve actually works, or why your Redis instance keeps crashing, or what would happen if you dropped a guy from 2049 into Ridgefield, Washington in 2024.

The conversations pile up. Thousands of them. Each one a fragment of your thinking — but scattered, unsearchable, forgotten the moment you close the tab.

So I built a tool that reads all of it.

---

## What 37 Million Words Look Like

The first thing the tool showed me: I'm two people.

My AI history splits almost perfectly in half — 1,058 conversations about AI and 1,025 about code. Then 768 about business, 703 about history, 267 about music, and a long tail of science, geopolitics, creative writing, and personal stuff.

But the real insight wasn't the categories. It was what **connected** them.

When I mapped the topics that appear across all my conversations, the same concepts kept showing up everywhere — in my code conversations, in my history deep-dives, in my business planning, even in my music discussions:

- **Supply chain** (652 conversations, 31 consecutive months)
- **Feedback loops** (358 conversations)
- **Pattern recognition** (353 conversations)
- **Power structures** (338 conversations)
- **Open source** (437 conversations)

I don't think about "topics." I think about **systems**. Supply chains aren't just logistics — they're the hidden skeleton of everything. Power structures aren't just politics — they're the operating system of civilization. Feedback loops aren't just engineering — they're how every complex thing maintains or destroys itself.

I've been running the same mental operating system across every domain for three years. I just didn't know it until I could see all the conversations at once.

---

## The Convergence

The tool found something I hadn't noticed: 49 conversations where two of my deepest threads collide.

I have a framework I call **Codex Vivus** — a "living codex" for understanding and navigating the systems that actually run the world. And I have another thread called **Iron Vein** — supply chain intelligence, what breaks and what replaces it.

In 49 S-tier conversations, these two frameworks appear together. Not because I planned it — because my thinking kept arriving at the same intersection from different directions. The supply chains that move atoms and the power structures that move people are **the same architecture viewed from different angles**.

The tool showed me that I'd been writing a book without knowing it.
